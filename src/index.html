Croatian hosts must report every overnight guest to eVisitor. To save your host time, fill in your details once below and send the resulting file to them. They'll add the property and tax bits and upload it. Nothing is ever sent through this page — when you close the tab, the form clears.

  3. 3 Send it to your host By email, WhatsApp, anything. They'll do the rest.

          Privacy
          
What happens to your data

        


        

          

            
While you use this page

            
    
              
  • Nothing leaves your browser. No data is sent to a server, ever. There is no backend.
    • 
              
  • No cookies, no tracking, no analytics. No third-party scripts run on this page.
    • 
              
  • It gets zapped when you close the tab. The only data we remember between visits is, if you're a host, your property defaults — so you don't have to retype them. You can clear them at any time with the button below.
    • 
              
  • The file is yours. When you save the file, it goes only to your device. Nobody else sees it unless you choose to share it.
    • 
            

            
          


          

            
What eVisitor collects, once uploaded

            

              eVisitor is run by the Croatian National Tourist Board. Every accommodation provider is required by
              law (Zakon o boravišnoj pristojbi; Zakon o pružanju usluga u turizmu) to register every overnight
              guest. For each guest, eVisitor records:
            

            
    
              
  • Full name and sex
    • 
              
  • Date and place of birth (country and city)
    • 
              
  • Citizenship
    • 
              
  • Address of residence (country, city, street)
    • 
              
  • Travel document type and number
    • 
              
  • Date and time of arrival and (foreseen) departure
    • 
              
  • Tourist tax category that applies
    • 
              
  • How the guest arrived (organised, individual, etc.)
    • 
            

            

              That data is shared with the Ministry of the Interior (MUP) for border-tracking purposes and with the
              local tourist board for tourist-tax accounting. For the official details, see
              evisitor.hr and the
              eVisitor wiki.
